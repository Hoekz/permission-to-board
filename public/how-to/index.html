How to play Permission to Board

Playing Together

Create the Game

You can create a game by choosing the 'Create New Game' button on the main page. This will allow you to choose your token color and then provides you with a game key that you can share with the other players.

Display the Game

For a better experience, Permission to Board provides a way to show the game state on a larger screen that shows the routes played, the current scores, active player, and the deck. Additionally, the game key is displayed so that others can simply look at the shared screen to join. This mode can be activated using the 'Host Existing Game' button and entering the game key.

Join the Game

Once a game has been created, other players can join on their devices by selecting the 'Join Existing Game' button and entering the game key. They will need to wait for the creator of the game to start play.

Playing Separate

Be sure to distribute the game key to all those who want to play!

The only real difference when playing separate is that anyone who wants to have a separate display view of the board will want to do so themselves.

Game Rules

The order of players is randomly determined and you will receive an indicator on your screen when it is your turn.

First Turn

The first turn of every player is to select 2 or 3 of the presented route cards. Your task will be to connect the 2 cities listed with your trains. If you do so, at the end of the game you will be rewarded the amount of points on the card. If you do not, You will lose the amount of points on the card.

On Your Turn

On your turn, you may perform one of the following actions:

Take 2 Train Cards

You can take 2 Train cards, either face up or from the draw pile. You may not choose a revealed Rainbow Train card when choosing 2 cards.

Take a Rainbow Train

If a Rainbow Train card has been revealed in the 5 face up Train cards, you may take it as your turn. If at any point 3 Rainbow Train cards have been revealed, the displayed cards are discarded and redealt.

Play a Route

You may choose a route from the map by tapping it, which will reveal the requirements for playing that route. If you have enough Train cards (either of the single color needed or the color needed plus Rainbow Train cards) you may play the route. Gray routes may be played with any single color. If there are parallel routes between 2 cities, you cannot play both of them.

Take More Route Cards

In the event that you want to earn more points at the end of the game, you may take more route cards. You can do this by viewing your routes and selecting the plus sign. This will present you with 3 new routes to choose from. You must choose at least 1 one of the new routes.

Scoring

There are 3 ways to score: Playing trains, completing routes, and having the longest continuous train.

Each time you play a route between 2 cities, you will be given points based off the length of the train:

  • 1 Train - 1 Point
  • 2 Trains - 2 Points
  • 3 Trains - 4 Points
  • 4 Trains - 7 Point
  • 5 Trains - 10 Points
  • 6 Trains - 15 Points

The player(s) at the end of the game with the longest continuous train will be rewarded 10 bonus points.
